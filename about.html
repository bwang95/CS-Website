About Me

I'm Brian Wang, an undergraduate student studying computer science at the University of Texas at Austin.

I took my first formal computer science course in my freshman year in high school. From there, I began competing in UIL-style competitions (UIL stands for University Interscholastic League for those unfamiliar with the term). Consequently, my choice of major was a simple one. I would study computer science to further my knowledge of the subject.

I enjoy travel and have been fortunate enough to live in a couple of foreign countries. In addition, I've visited quite a few, including Hong Kong, Australia, and the UK.

I'm currently interning at Square for the summer. I also used to work on the TasteBud app at Azul Mobile; you can check them out by clicking here.

In Spring 2015, I worked with Austin Free-Net to develop the Austin Free-Net On the Go application. This application offers learning modules to help users with technical literacy and serves as a point of contact for the organization. It's currently available on Google Play. The source code is available under the MIT license on GitHub.

Cooking is another one of my pastimes. The end result may not always be great, but I do enjoy trying new recipes. The photo to the right documents one of the more successful attempts.

You can always find out more about my academic activities and experience by viewing my Resume, accessible through the site navigation menu.
